A few data from the surveys,

Pemba
365 fishers, 11 beach seines (2% of the fishers), 44 ringnets (14% of the fishers)

TACMP
144 fishers, 0 beach seines reported but known to happen. 

**So far** we have identified the following,

*Access restriction* in Pemba,
- Creation of the new MCA that will reduce access to ringnets. Of the interviewed fishers, 14% reported being ring netters. We estimate from fishery patterns mapping that less than 10% of their fishing grounds will be affected

*Mitigation* in Pemba,
- measures to reduce post-harvest loss on sardine fishers (which can be between 20% and 50% in coastal communities) with sardine drying racks and other interventions.
- Credit and savings groups in the communities with the largest number of sardine fishers

*Access restriction* in TACMP,
- we are planning to support strengthening the enforcement of core zones in the park. They currently cover 1.5 sqkm or approximately 1% of the reef areas in TACMP.

*Mitigation* in TACMP,
- credit and savings groups in the communities in the south, as these have been identified as the ones mostly fishing near the core-zones
- development of octopus closures in target communities
