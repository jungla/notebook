Guest of honor, MPRU manager, représentatives of local authorities, communities, partners and colleagues, habari na machana.

First, I'd like to thank all the participants for **attending this event in person**. This is an important moment for WCS and MPRU as it **materially represents** the product of our collaboration in the past few years.

WCS and MPRU have been committed to protecting the important ecosystems of the Pemba channel, which is **an area blessed by unique a geography and abundant biodiversity**.  Here we can find extensive mangrove forests, seagrass beds and **some of the most resilient coral reefs in the world.** 

The **Pemba channel is a unique oceanographic feature** that helps maintaining the waters around Pemba and Tanga cool thus protecting the coral reefs from extreme climate events making it **one of the 50 most important coral reef refuges in the world**.

Nevertheless, this is an area that is under **increased pressure**, through coastal development, expanding fisheries and illegal and desctructive fishing practices. Costal communities depend on the marine resources for their livelihoods and it is critical that effective and sustainable management of the ecosystems is ensured.

The **MPAs in this region, TACMP and TMRS, play a critical role** to support the fisheries and the biodiversity and it is essential for the future of these ecosystems and communities that enough capacity, material and technical is available to managers and decision makers.

This ranger station and the demarcation of the reserves are **two of the most visible contributions** that WCS and Blue Action Fund supported, but they are not the only ones, and arguably not the most important either.

I would like here to also highlight **the work that MPRU and WCS have done in the past years** to develop both the Strategic Plan for MPRU and the general management plan for the TMRS. Both these documents are the **backbone** of a management strategy for the TMRS and the other MPAs and reserves.

The infrastructures presented here, together with the support with equipment and training for monitoring control and surveillance, and ecological monitoring are **actions in a broader strategy** of support of the MPA as delineated in the GMP.

**These are not the final outcomes**, but tools that we hope can help achieve the real objective of having the **marine ecosystems and resources in the Tanga region preserved and well-managed.**

**We hope to continue our collaboration in the future** and to be here to **witness** the improvements that we wish to see in the status of the reefs and the communities that depend on them.

I would the like to **conclude with a good news**.
Today, if all goes well, we will be formally signing the agreement with Blue Action Fund to support further activities in the Tanga region for 4 more years.

As you know we have been working together in the past months to develop a project to support TACMP with infrastructure, demarcation, coral reef restoration and capacity development and we are now ready to start the work. Together, just as we did in the TMRS.

With this I'd like to conclude and **to thank you again for your continuos support throughout this work**. In particular I'd like to personally thank Johnson and Magreth for arranging this nice event and ensuring that our collaboration is strong and effective.
